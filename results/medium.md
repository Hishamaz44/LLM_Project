**Against Debater**

Holding them liable is not censorship; it is accountability for their distribution systems. Voluntary self-regulation has failed. Only legal liability will incentivize platforms to redesign their algorithms for accuracy over outrage, protecting public discourse and saving lives.

**Against Debater**

Imposing legal liability on platforms for user-generated misinformation is a dangerous path that threatens the foundation of the free internet.

Second, defining "misinformation" is inherently subjective. Legally deputizing private tech monopolies to act as arbiters of truth invites government pressure to suppress disfavored speech, violating democratic principles. 

Instead of destroying free expression through liability, we must combat misinformation through media literacy and transparent algorithmic design.

**Against Debater**

CRISPR embryo editing is unethical due to severe, irreversible risks. Off-target mutations can introduce novel, heritable genetic defects, permanently altering the human gene pool. Furthermore, safe alternatives like Pre-implantation Genetic Diagnosis (PGD) already exist to screen embryos, making this dangerous intervention medically unnecessary. Opponents raise safety concerns, but rigorous preclinical research demonstrates that CRISPR’s accuracy is rapidly advancing, minimizing off-target effects.
